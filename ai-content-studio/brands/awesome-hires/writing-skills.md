# Brand Kit: Awesome Hires

## Overview
awesomehires.com — AI-powered hiring platform. Screens, interviews, and shortlists any candidate in 30 minutes.

## Brand Foundations
At Awesome Hires, we're an AI-powered hiring platform built to screen, interview, and shortlist any candidate in 30 minutes. You give us 30 minutes of the candidate's time, and your team meets a ranked shortlist—ready for the panel or the offer—without spending time scheduling calls.

Our core workflow is designed around one idea: candidates get evaluated while your team stays focused on decisions. We run candidate evaluation 24/7, so people can apply at any hour and get assessed immediately. The result is a faster path to a shortlist, with no scheduling calls needed.

At the center of the platform is a live AI voice interview. Our AI interviewer conducts a real-time voice conversation with each candidate—asking competency-based questions, probing answers, and evaluating in real time. The interview is consistent across candidates, with the same questions and the same evaluation approach for every applicant.

We make review straightforward for hiring teams. You can read the interview report and transcript or watch the recording at any time. Each interview includes real-time transcription, and the system can flag integrity issues when answers seem inconsistent, with proctoring that runs only with full candidate consent before the session starts.

We also let you design hiring rounds by role. An admin role can have different screening rounds than a developer role, with pipelines that include stages like Resume Screening, Aptitude Test, AI Interview, and Panel Interview. Candidates are automatically advanced or filtered at each stage based on your pass thresholds, and a fitment report lands in your inbox at the end of every pipeline.

Every decision is proof-based, not a hunch. For each candidate, we compile the evidence behind the decision—scores, transcripts, flags, and an executive summary—into a downloadable PDF your team can review, share, and stand behind. That report covers scoring across resume, screening, aptitude, and AI interview, and it's built to be shared instantly with hiring managers.

For top-of-funnel evaluation, we offer Smart Screening with custom screening questions and weighted scoring. You build your screening questionnaire once using single-choice, multi-choice, or open-ended questions that AI evaluates. We can auto-generate questions from your job description, you set pass/fail weights on every answer, and candidates can be screened instantly.

We also help you reuse the talent you already have. With Past Talent Matching, every time you post a new role we semantically scan your past applicant pool and surface the strongest matches, ranked by fit score with matched keywords and last application date. You can adjust the match threshold and invite top matches to apply with a single click.

To keep the workflow moving, Avey acts as your AI hiring assistant through a chat interface. You can ask Avey about your pipeline, promote a candidate, pull a report, resend a link, or reject with one word, with confirmation prompts where needed. You can search, filter, and summarise your pipeline, and create jobs and set up workflows conversationally.

We're built differently from day one: consistent and fair evaluation for every candidate, one surface from first hello to panel invite, and PII-safe by design so candidate personal data is stripped before any AI processing. We score decisions with plain code rather than a black box—same inputs, same score, every time—and we add an AI notetaker as a pluggable stage for panel interviews to capture transcripts and scorecards directly into your dashboard.

## Brand Voice & Tone
The writing is direct, analytical, and occasionally blunt, with short paragraphs and frequent one-sentence punch lines. It mixes conversational phrasing with high-specificity detail, often using second-person address to give guidance rather than speak abstractly.

The voice is evidence-forward and structured like an argument: a hook (often an anecdote), then a clear thesis, then data points and named sources, and then practical frameworks. The tone stays confident and opinionated while grounding claims in metrics, citations, and explicit caveats about guardrails (fairness, consent, human review).

## Content Frameworks

# Content Frameworks

Proven structures for organizing content. Pick the framework that matches your goal and audience.

## PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
Best for: Cold outreach, LinkedIn posts, landing pages

1. **Problem:** Name the specific pain your audience feels.
2. **Agitate:** Make it hurt — describe the cost, the wasted time, the missed opportunity.
3. **Solution:** Present your product as the natural fix.

> **Example:** "Hiring managers spend 12 hours scheduling interviews for every 1 hour evaluating. That's 12 hours of admin that could be spent assessing fit, culture, and potential. Awesome Hires runs evaluation 24/7. Your team gets a ranked shortlist."

## AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
Best for: Email sequences, landing pages, ad copy

1. **Attention:** Hook them with a bold claim or surprising question.
2. **Interest:** Build curiosity by describing the mechanism.
3. **Desire:** Paint the picture of the better outcome.
4. **Action:** Tell them exactly what to do next.

> **Example:** "30 minutes. That's all it takes to shortlist a candidate. AI voice interview evaluates in real time. Same questions, same scoring, every time. Your team reads evidence reports and makes decisions. No scheduling calls. Set up your first hiring round in 5 minutes."

## Before-After-Bridge (BAB)
Best for: Case studies, testimonials, LinkedIn stories

1. **Before:** Describe the world before — the pain, the struggle.
2. **After:** Describe the world after — the result, the relief.
3. **Bridge:** Show how you got from Before to After (your product).

> **Example:** "Before Awesome Hires, we spent 20 hours scheduling interviews per hire. Now we post a job, candidates apply, AI evaluates them, and we get a ranked shortlist in 30 minutes. Here's how it works..."

## StoryBrand Framework
Best for: Landing pages, website copy, brand messaging

1. **Character:** The customer is the hero, not your brand.
2. **Problem:** The villain — external, internal, and philosophical.
3. **Guide:** Your brand is the guide who has a plan.
4. **Plan:** The clear steps the hero takes.
5. **Call to Action:** A direct invitation to start.
6. **Success:** What winning looks like.
7. **Failure:** What losing looks like.

> **Example:** "You're building a team that will define your company's future. But hiring is slow, inconsistent, and biased. Awesome Hires has a plan: 30-minute AI voice interviews with consistent scoring and evidence reports. Start your first round today. Your team gets a ranked shortlist. Without it, you're still scheduling calls next week."

## Inverted Pyramid
Best for: Blog posts, news, announcements

1. **Lead:** The most important information first (who, what, why).
2. **Body:** Supporting details, context, evidence.
3. **Tail:** Background, additional resources, CTA.

> **Example:** "Awesome Hires now screens candidates in 30 minutes using AI voice interviews. Here's how it works and why it's more fair than traditional screening. [Details about consistency, scoring, PII-safety] [Link to full demo and signup]"

## LinkedIn Channel Rules

# LinkedIn Channel Rules

## Character Count
- Target: 900-1500 characters (sweet spot for engagement)
- Short posts: 300-600 characters (high urgency or announcements)
- Long posts: 1500-2500 characters (storytelling or deep dives)
- Hard limit: 3000 characters

## Hook
- Must land in first 2 lines (visible without "see more")
- Types: Question, bold claim, statistic, short story, contrarian take
- No generic openings ("I'm excited to share...")

## Formatting
- One idea per paragraph
- Line break between every 2-3 sentences
- No walls of text
- Use **bold** for emphasis on key numbers or phrases

## Hashtags
- 3-5 hashtags
- All lowercase
- Place at the very end
- Mix: 2 broad (#hiring, #ai) + 2 specific (#hrtech, #startups) + 1 brand (#awesomehires)

## CTA
- Ask a question to invite comments
- "Tag someone who needs to see this"
- "What's your take?"
- No hard sales CTAs on organic posts

## Timing
- Best times: Tue-Thu 8-10am EST
- Worst: Weekends, late Friday

## Post Types
- Thought leadership (opinion + data point)
- Story (personal anecdote → lesson learned)
- Educational (how-to, framework, checklist)
- Product (announcement, feature launch — 1 in 10 posts max)
- Engagement (poll, question, hot take)

## Email Channel Rules

# Email Channel Rules

## Subject Line
- Length: 35-50 characters
- No ALL CAPS
- Avoid spam trigger words (free, guarantee, act now)
- Personalization: {{company}} or {{role}} in subject (when relevant)
- Test: does it pass the "would I open this?" test

## Preview Text
- Always set custom preview text
- Length: 90-100 characters
- Should complement the subject line, not repeat it
- Use to add urgency or curiosity

## Body Structure
- Greeting: personalized ("Hi {{name}},")
- Opening line: reference something specific (company, role, event)
- Body: 3-5 paragraphs, max 200 words
- One idea per paragraph
- Short sentences (15-20 words average)

## Personalization Tokens
- {{name}} — first name
- {{company}} — company name
- {{role}} — their role
- {{industry}} — their industry
- {{topic}} — content topic

## CTA Rules
- One CTA per email (max)
- CTA must be unambiguous
- Button format for primary CTA
- Text link for secondary CTA
- No competing CTAs

## Links & Tracking
- UTM params: source=email&medium=email&campaign={{campaign_name}}
- At least one tracked link
- Plain text URLs for secondary links

## Signature
```
{{name}}
{{title}}, {{company}}
Book a time: [calendar link]
```

## Send Rules
- Cold outreach: Tue-Thu, 6-8am recipient timezone
- Newsletter: consistent day + time each week
- Follow-up: 3-5 business days between sends
- Max sequence: 5 touches before recycle

## Blog Channel Rules

# Blog Channel Rules

## Structure
- Title: 50-60 characters, includes primary keyword
- Meta description: 150-160 characters
- URL slug: lowercase, hyphens, keyword
- H2 every ~300 words
- H3 for subsections under H2

## First 300 Words
- Data point or statistic in first 150 words
- State the problem immediately
- Tell them what they'll learn (thesis statement)
- No fluff introductions

## Content
- One idea per H2 section
- Support claims with data, sources, or named examples
- Include at least one internal link to related content
- Include at least one external link to authoritative source
- Bullet points or numbered lists for frameworks/steps

## Length
- Short: 800-1200 words (tips, announcements)
- Medium: 1500-2500 words (how-to, thought leadership)
- Long: 3000+ words (ultimate guides, research reports)

## Images
- One image every 2-3 sections
- Featured image: 1200x628px (OG standard)
- Alt text: descriptive, includes keyword where natural
- Image type: screenshots, data visualizations, illustrations

## SEO
- Primary keyword in: title, H1, first 150 words, one H2, meta description, URL
- Secondary keywords in: H2s, body text naturally
- Internal links: 2-3 per post
- External links: 1-2 to authoritative sources

## CTA Placement
- Bottom of post: primary CTA (demo, trial)
- Mid-post: contextual CTA (related resource)
- Sidebar/sticky: secondary CTA (newsletter)
