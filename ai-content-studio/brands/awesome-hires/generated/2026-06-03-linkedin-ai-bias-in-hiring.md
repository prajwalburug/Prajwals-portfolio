## Variant a (direct)

Most hiring teams are building AI bias in hiring into their process without realizing it. Here's the data.

The pattern is clear across every dataset we've examined: the tools aren't inherently biased — the training data is.

When an AI screening model is trained on historical hires from a team that's 80% one demographic, it learns to prefer that demographic. It's not malice. It's math.

Here's what works instead:
• Audit training data for representation gaps before deployment
• Use deterministic scoring rules that are transparent and testable
• Strip PII before any AI evaluation — name, age, location shouldn't factor

The fix isn't less AI. It's better data hygiene and auditability.

## Variant b (storytelling)

A CTO told me: 'Our AI rejected every qualified candidate last quarter.' That's not a bug. It's AI bias in hiring.

We sat down with his team to understand why AI bias in hiring was producing skewed results. The root cause wasn't the algorithm. It was the historical data — trained on years of hires from an industry that's been overwhelmingly homogeneous.

They rebuilt the screening criteria from scratch. Same AI engine. New scoring rules. Within 60 days, their pipeline diversity improved by 3x at the shortlist stage.

The before and after wasn't about changing the model. It was about changing what the model was trained to value.

Technical debt in hiring takes many forms. Biased training data is the most expensive kind.
