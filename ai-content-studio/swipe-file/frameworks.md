# Content Frameworks

Proven structures for organizing content. Pick the framework that matches your goal and audience.

## PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
Best for: Cold outreach, LinkedIn posts, landing pages

1. **Problem:** Name the specific pain your audience feels.
2. **Agitate:** Make it hurt — describe the cost, the wasted time, the missed opportunity.
3. **Solution:** Present your product as the natural fix.

> **Example:** "Hiring managers spend 12 hours scheduling interviews for every 1 hour evaluating. That's 12 hours of admin that could be spent assessing fit, culture, and potential. Awesome Hires runs evaluation 24/7. Your team gets a ranked shortlist."

## AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
Best for: Email sequences, landing pages, ad copy

1. **Attention:** Hook them with a bold claim or surprising question.
2. **Interest:** Build curiosity by describing the mechanism.
3. **Desire:** Paint the picture of the better outcome.
4. **Action:** Tell them exactly what to do next.

> **Example:** "30 minutes. That's all it takes to shortlist a candidate. AI voice interview evaluates in real time. Same questions, same scoring, every time. Your team reads evidence reports and makes decisions. No scheduling calls. Set up your first hiring round in 5 minutes."

## Before-After-Bridge (BAB)
Best for: Case studies, testimonials, LinkedIn stories

1. **Before:** Describe the world before — the pain, the struggle.
2. **After:** Describe the world after — the result, the relief.
3. **Bridge:** Show how you got from Before to After (your product).

> **Example:** "Before Awesome Hires, we spent 20 hours scheduling interviews per hire. Now we post a job, candidates apply, AI evaluates them, and we get a ranked shortlist in 30 minutes. Here's how it works..."

## StoryBrand Framework
Best for: Landing pages, website copy, brand messaging

1. **Character:** The customer is the hero, not your brand.
2. **Problem:** The villain — external, internal, and philosophical.
3. **Guide:** Your brand is the guide who has a plan.
4. **Plan:** The clear steps the hero takes.
5. **Call to Action:** A direct invitation to start.
6. **Success:** What winning looks like.
7. **Failure:** What losing looks like.

> **Example:** "You're building a team that will define your company's future. But hiring is slow, inconsistent, and biased. Awesome Hires has a plan: 30-minute AI voice interviews with consistent scoring and evidence reports. Start your first round today. Your team gets a ranked shortlist. Without it, you're still scheduling calls next week."

## Inverted Pyramid
Best for: Blog posts, news, announcements

1. **Lead:** The most important information first (who, what, why).
2. **Body:** Supporting details, context, evidence.
3. **Tail:** Background, additional resources, CTA.

> **Example:** "Awesome Hires now screens candidates in 30 minutes using AI voice interviews. Here's how it works and why it's more fair than traditional screening. [Details about consistency, scoring, PII-safety] [Link to full demo and signup]"
